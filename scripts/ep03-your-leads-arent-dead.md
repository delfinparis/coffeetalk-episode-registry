# Coffee Talk with Tim & D.J.
## Episode 3: "Your Leads Aren't Dead. You Buried Them Alive."
**Air Date:** 2026-02-24 | **Runtime:** 15 minutes | **Format:** Live stream | **Tone:** Two buddies having coffee, dropping truth bombs

---

## COLD OPEN (0:00 – ~0:45)

**D.J.:** (sets down coffee) 48 percent.

**TIM:** 48 percent of what?

**D.J.:** That's the number of real estate agents who never — not once — follow up with a lead after the first attempt.

**TIM:** Half of agents ghost their own leads?

**D.J.:** Half of agents ghost their own leads. And then wonder why their conversion rate is so low.

**TIM:** (shaking head) Okay. I already don't like where this Coffee Talk is going.

**D.J.:** I'm D.J. Paris, that's Tim Littleton, welcome to Coffee Talk. Today we're going to show you why your "dead leads" probably aren't dead — and exactly how many times you need to follow up before you've actually earned the right to move on. Stick around. This one's going to sting a little.

---

## SEGMENT 1: THE PROBLEM (~0:45 – ~3:30)

**D.J.:** So here's the full picture. 48% of agents quit after one follow-up attempt. One. They call, they don't reach someone, they move on.

**TIM:** Which... I mean, I get it. You're busy. Next lead.

**D.J.:** Totally understand the instinct. Here's what those agents don't know: it takes between 5 and 12 touches to convert the average lead.

**TIM:** Wait. Five to twelve?

**D.J.:** Five to twelve. Calls, texts, emails, voicemails — a combination of all of it. Before that person is ready to say "yes, you're my agent."

**TIM:** And half of agents make one attempt and walk away.

**D.J.:** One attempt. And here's what makes it brutal. NAR data says 70% of buyers hire the first agent they actually speak to.

**TIM:** So it's not about who finds them first. It's about who reaches them first.

**D.J.:** Exactly. You could be lead number one in their inbox. But if you call once and give up, and some other agent calls three days later — that agent gets the business.

**TIM:** (leaning back) So the leads aren't bad. The agents are just... not following through.

**D.J.:** The leads are converting. They're buying homes right now. Just not with the agent who stopped at one touch and called them a "dead lead."

**TIM:** That's a painful sentence.

**D.J.:** Your "dead leads" didn't die. You buried them alive and handed the shovel to your competition.

**TIM:** (pause) Okay that one's going to stick with me. So what's actually happening here? Why are agents quitting so early?

**D.J.:** That's exactly what we're getting into. But first — if you just joined us — here's what we're talking about. Half of agents ghost their own leads after one attempt. It takes 5 to 12 touches to convert. The math is ugly. Stay with us.

---

## SEGMENT 2: WHY IT HAPPENS (~3:30 – ~6:30)

**D.J.:** Three reasons agents quit too early. First one: they're afraid of being annoying.

**TIM:** Which is — okay, I feel that. Nobody wants to be that agent. The one blowing up someone's phone.

**D.J.:** Completely valid fear. Wrong conclusion. Here's the thing — if 48% of agents quit after one attempt, your third follow-up isn't annoying. It's remarkable. You're doing something almost nobody does.

**TIM:** You're standing out by just... showing up again.

**D.J.:** Right. The second reason: no system. They're running on memory and good intentions. "Oh, I should call that person back." And then three weeks go by.

**TIM:** The road to a dead pipeline is paved with good intentions.

**D.J.:** (laughing) That's exactly it. And the third reason is the sneaky one. They're measuring the wrong thing. They look at a lead and ask "is this lead good or bad?" instead of asking "have I done enough to reach this person?"

**TIM:** They're blaming the lead instead of the process.

**D.J.:** Blaming the lead is comfortable. Auditing your own follow-up process is uncomfortable.

**TIM:** So what does an agent actually do about it?

**D.J.:** Okay, here's where we're going to stop and do something right now. Before we get to the system, I need everyone watching to do one thing. Open your CRM, your phone contacts, wherever you track leads — right now, while we talk.

**TIM:** Do it. We'll wait.

**D.J.:** Scroll back through the last 90 days. Find one lead — just one — where you made one or two attempts, didn't reach them, and wrote them off. Somebody you labeled "dead" or "no response" or just stopped thinking about.

**TIM:** (sipping coffee) Find the person you gave up on.

**D.J.:** Got them? Write their name down. Put it somewhere you can see it. Because in about four minutes, we're going to do something with that name that might make you some money.

**TIM:** I like where this is going.

**D.J.:** The system for making sure you never bury a live lead again — that's next.

---

## SEGMENT 3: THE SYSTEM/SOLUTION (~6:30 – ~11:00)

**D.J.:** Alright. The framework is called The 5-Touch Floor. The idea is simple: no lead gets labeled "dead" until they've gotten at least five meaningful contact attempts. That's the floor. Not the ceiling.

**TIM:** Five is the minimum before you can call it.

**D.J.:** Exactly. Here's how the five touches break down.

Touch One: Same-day call. The moment a lead comes in, you call. Doesn't matter if it's 3pm on a Tuesday or 8am on a Saturday. MIT did a lead response study — leads contacted within five minutes are 21 times more likely to convert than leads contacted after 30 minutes.

**TIM:** 21 times. Not 21 percent more. 21 times.

**D.J.:** 21 times. So Touch One is fast. Same day, ideally within the hour.

**TIM:** Okay, what if they don't pick up? Which is... most of the time.

**D.J.:** Touch Two, within the same day: text. Not a paragraph. Not a pitch. Something like: "Hey, just tried giving you a call — happy to chat whenever works for you." That's it. You're a human being, not a marketing email.

**TIM:** Short. No pressure. Got it. What's Touch Three?

**D.J.:** Touch Three is the next morning. Email. And this is where most agents blow it — they send a template. "Hi, my name is, I'd love to be your agent, call me anytime." Delete.

**TIM:** I've gotten that email. I have deleted that email.

**D.J.:** Instead, send something with actual value. A market update for their zip code. A list of homes that fit what they asked about. Something that proves you listened and you did your homework.

**TIM:** So you're earning the next conversation instead of demanding it.

**D.J.:** Exactly. Now Touch Four — this is three days later. You call again, but at a different time of day than Touch One. If you called at noon before, try 8am or 5:30pm. People's schedules vary. You're not stalking them, you're being strategic.

**TIM:** Different time, different chance they pick up.

**D.J.:** And Touch Five — this is the one that gets people. It's the break-up text.

**TIM:** (laughing) The break-up text?

**D.J.:** Five words, give or take. "Should I stop reaching out?" That's it.

**TIM:** Wait, seriously?

**D.J.:** Seriously. Because it does two things. One — it respects their time and gives them an out. Two — it triggers a response from people who were interested but kept getting busy. You'd be stunned how many people reply "No! Sorry, things have been crazy, can we talk Thursday?"

**TIM:** So sometimes the fifth touch is the one that opens the door.

**D.J.:** Five touches is your floor. Under five, you don't get to call it dead. Okay — remember that name you wrote down?

**TIM:** I've got mine.

**D.J.:** That person is on Touch Zero right now. You ghosted them. They may not be dead. So here's what we're going to do right now. Pull up your messages. I'm going to give you the exact text to send. Ready?

**TIM:** Give it to them.

**D.J.:** Here it is. "Hey [Name] — reached out a while back, just wanted to check in. Still thinking about [buying/selling]? No pressure either way." That's it. Send it before we get to the next segment.

**TIM:** Send it right now. For real — we're not moving on until you do it.

**D.J.:** (beat) And when you send it, drop "ALIVE" in the comments. Let's see how many leads we collectively resurrect today.

**TIM:** (sips coffee, waits) I love this.

---

## SEGMENT 4: THE HARD OBJECTION (~11:00 – ~12:45)

**TIM:** Okay, I have to push back on something. Because I know what people watching are thinking.

**D.J.:** Go ahead.

**TIM:** Five touches sounds great on paper. But at some point — like, three voicemails in a week — you are being annoying. You are that agent. I don't care what the stats say.

**D.J.:** Fair pushback. And here's the distinction that makes the whole thing work: there's a difference between five touches in five days and five touches over five weeks.

**TIM:** Okay. Explain that.

**D.J.:** The cadence matters as much as the count. Touch One and Two — same day, right? That's appropriate. Someone just raised their hand. But Touch Three is the next morning. Touch Four is three days after that. Touch Five is a week after that. You're not blowing up their phone. You're showing professional persistence over about two weeks.

**TIM:** That's... actually really different from what I was picturing.

**D.J.:** And here's the other thing. Each touch has to deliver something. It can't just be "hey, just checking in again." That IS annoying. But "here are three homes that hit the market this week in your price range" — that's service.

**TIM:** You're not chasing them. You're bringing them something every time.

**D.J.:** Right. The specific tool I'd use for this: Follow Up Boss. It lets you build out those exact cadences, set automatic reminders, and track where every lead is in the sequence. You're not relying on memory anymore — the system tells you when and how to reach out. And each touch gets logged so you actually know when you've hit the 5-Touch Floor.

**TIM:** So it's not willpower. It's infrastructure.

**D.J.:** Willpower fails. Systems don't sleep.

---

## SEGMENT 5: THE MATH/MOTIVATION (~12:45 – ~14:15)

**D.J.:** Let's put real numbers on this. Because I want it to be concrete.

Average commission on a home sale in this market — call it $10,000 to $12,000. Let's say $10,000 to be conservative.

**TIM:** Okay.

**D.J.:** Now think about the last 12 months. How many leads did you get that you contacted once or twice and wrote off?

**TIM:** If I'm honest? Probably 20, 30. Maybe more.

**D.J.:** Studies suggest that about 5% of those leads — if they received a proper 5-touch sequence — would have converted. So out of 20 leads you abandoned early, that's one deal. One deal you handed to a competitor.

**TIM:** $10,000.

**D.J.:** $10,000. But most agents aren't abandoning 20 leads. They're abandoning a lot more. And it compounds. Do this for five years at that rate — you're looking at $50,000 in commissions you paid forward to whoever was willing to make that third call.

**TIM:** That's not a lead problem. That's a retirement account problem.

**D.J.:** Here's the flip side. If you implement the 5-Touch Floor starting this week — on your current leads and the "dead" ones from the last 90 days — even recovering one deal from your abandoned pile pays for every CRM subscription you'll ever have. Twice over.

**TIM:** The system pays for itself the first time it works.

**D.J.:** The first time. The leads are sitting there. Some of them are still interested. They just need one more human being to show up and ask.

---

## CLOSE (~14:15 – ~15:00)

**TIM:** Alright — before we wrap, give them the three things. What do they do today?

**D.J.:** Three things. First: audit the last 90 days. Pull up every lead you wrote off and count how many touches they actually got. If it's under five, they're not dead — they're just waiting.

Second: build the 5-Touch Floor into your process. Five touches, spaced out, each one with something of value. Use a CRM to automate the reminders so it's not on your memory.

Third — and you should have already done this — send that text to the person you wrote down earlier. If you haven't sent it yet, do it the second we sign off.

**TIM:** Be the agent who shows up five times. Because 48% won't even show up twice.

**D.J.:** (nods) That's the identity shift right there. Most agents quit before they ever get to the conversation. You don't have to be the best agent in your market. You just have to be the one who kept calling.

**TIM:** That's genuinely actionable.

**D.J.:** Your leads aren't dead. You just stopped calling.

**TIM:** That's the show. Thanks for hanging out with us — we'll see you next time.

**D.J.:** Go make some calls.
