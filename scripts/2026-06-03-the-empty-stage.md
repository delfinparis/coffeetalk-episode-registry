## SCRIPT

### COLD OPEN (0:00 – ~0:45)

**D.J.:** *(setting down coffee, straight to camera)* Eighty-five percent.

**TIM:** Eighty-five percent of what?

**D.J.:** Eighty-five percent of real estate agents aren't on the single biggest stage in America right now. And more than a hundred million people walk past that stage every single day.

**TIM:** A hundred million. Daily?

**D.J.:** Daily. I'm D.J. Paris, that's Tim Littleton, and here's the part that's going to bug you. The room is packed. The competition is almost gone. And most agents are standing outside, telling themselves it's not for them. Stay right here. We're going to walk you onto that stage today.

### SEGMENT 1: THE PROBLEM (~0:45 – ~3:30)

**TIM:** Okay, you can't open with a hundred million and just keep sipping coffee. What's the stage?

**D.J.:** TikTok.

**TIM:** *(sits back)* See, the second you said it, I felt half our audience reach for the scroll button.

**D.J.:** And that reflex right there is the whole episode. Let me give you the number that matters. NAR's twenty twenty-four Technology Survey asked agents which platforms they use for their business. TikTok came in at fifteen percent.

**TIM:** Fifteen. So basically nobody.

**D.J.:** Fewer than two in ten. Compare that to Facebook, where almost nine in ten agents are crowded in, all shouting at the same people.

**TIM:** So everybody piled into the room where it's hardest to be heard.

**D.J.:** They piled into the loud room. Meanwhile, the same survey puts Instagram in the low sixties, LinkedIn under half, YouTube at twenty-five percent. TikTok's down at the bottom. And here's what makes that bottom number a gift instead of a warning.

**TIM:** Go.

**D.J.:** More than a hundred million Americans open TikTok every day. That's not a teenager number anymore. That's a national-television-sized audience, and it's sitting in your pocket.

**TIM:** But D.J., if I'm being the agent in the comments right now, I'm thinking, that's a hundred million people watching dance videos, not buying houses.

**D.J.:** Hold that exact thought. We're coming back for it, because it's the most expensive sentence in real estate right now.

**TIM:** *(laughing)* The most expensive sentence. Okay.

**D.J.:** An empty stage in front of a packed room. That's not a problem. That's the best setup you'll ever get. Most agents just can't see it.

### SEGMENT 2: WHY IT HAPPENS (~3:30 – ~6:30)

**TIM:** So why is it empty? A hundred million people, and agents won't touch it. That's strange.

**D.J.:** Three reasons, and you'll recognize all three in yourself. First one. We think it's the wrong crowd. "My clients aren't on there." We'll handle that one head-on in a few minutes, because the data tells a different story.

**TIM:** I want to come back to that, because I've said it out loud.

**D.J.:** Everyone has. Second reason. We think it has to be polished. Agents picture trending sounds, jump cuts, a whole production, and they decide they don't have time. So they make nothing.

**TIM:** Right, perfect becomes the excuse for zero.

**D.J.:** Every time. Third reason, and this is the real one. It feels embarrassing to talk to an empty room. You post, you get four views, three of them are your mom, and you quit.

**TIM:** *(laughing)* My mom is very supportive.

**D.J.:** As she should be. But here's what I want you to do right now, and don't skip this. Think of one question a client actually asked you this week. A real one. "Is now a dumb time to buy?" "Should I waive the inspection?" Whatever it was. Don't type it in the comments yet. Just hold that one question in your head.

**TIM:** I've got mine.

**D.J.:** Good. Keep it right there. We're going to do something with that question before we sign off, and it's going to take you about thirty seconds.

**TIM:** So the room's empty because it feels weird, it feels like work, and we've convinced ourselves nobody good is watching.

**D.J.:** And every one of those is fixable. The fix is stupidly simple. That's almost why people don't trust it.

### SEGMENT 3: THE SYSTEM/SOLUTION (~6:30 – ~11:00)

**D.J.:** So here's the system. I call it the Empty Stage Method, and it's three moves. Anybody can run it with the phone they're holding right now.

**TIM:** Three moves. Lay out the first one.

**D.J.:** Move one. Answer, don't announce. Most agents get on camera and announce themselves. "Hi, I'm your local expert, call me for all your real estate needs." Nobody watches that. Nobody. Instead, you take that question you're holding in your head, the real one a client asked you, and you just answer it. Out loud. To the camera. Like the person's sitting across the table from you. That's the whole video. One question, one honest answer, thirty seconds.

**TIM:** So I'm not making content. I'm answering a client. I just happen to be filming it.

**D.J.:** That's exactly it. The work's already done. You answer that question fifteen times a week on the phone. Now one of those answers gets seen by strangers who have the same question.

**TIM:** Okay. Move two.

**D.J.:** Move two. Plant the flag. Make it local. Your town, your streets, your numbers. "Here's what a three-bedroom actually costs in Oak Park this month." "Here's the one block in town where prices didn't drop." TikTok hands local content to local people. When you name the place, the algorithm knows exactly who to show it to.

**TIM:** So the smaller and more specific I go, the better it works? That feels backwards.

**D.J.:** It feels backwards and it's the most important thing I'll say today. You're not trying to go viral in California. You're trying to be the one agent who keeps showing up when somebody in your zip code can't sleep at two in the morning and starts thumbing through TikTok wondering if they can afford to move.

**TIM:** *(nodding)* That person's real. I've gotten that call.

**D.J.:** Everybody's gotten that call. Now you can be the face they already trust when they make it. And that brings us to move three. Reps over ring lights. Three posts a week. Shot on your phone, no edit, posted before you talk yourself out of it. Done beats perfect every time, and on an empty stage, done beats everybody.

**TIM:** That question I'm holding. When do I actually get to use it?

**D.J.:** Almost time. One more thing and then I'm going to put you to work on it. Stay with me.

*(beat)*

**D.J.:** The reason these three moves work isn't that they're clever. It's that the eighty-five percent who skipped this platform left the door wide open. You're not fighting for attention. You're walking onto a stage nobody else will stand on.

**TIM:** And while we're talking, somebody watching is thinking it. Drop it in the comments. What's the one question your clients keep asking you? Put it down right now. That's your first video, and you don't even have to think of it.

### SEGMENT 4: THE HARD OBJECTION (~11:00 – ~12:45)

**TIM:** Alright. I held off, but I have to say the thing half this audience is thinking, and I half-believe it myself. My clients aren't on TikTok. My buyers and sellers are grown adults with money. They're not on there watching kids dance. So why am I performing for an audience that'll never hire me?

**D.J.:** That's the sentence I told you to hold onto. And it's wrong, and I can show you why. That hundred million people a day isn't a high school. TikTok's US monthly audience is somewhere around a hundred and thirty-six million people, and the fastest-growing slice of it for years has been grown adults. **[STAT NEEDED: share of US TikTok users age 30 and older — suggested source: Pew Research Center, Social Media Use 2025, or DataReportal Digital 2025 US]**

**TIM:** So the "it's all teenagers" picture is just old.

**D.J.:** It's a snapshot from twenty nineteen that never got updated. But here's the part that closes the case even if you don't believe a word of that. Open TikTok right now and search your own town's name plus "real estate." Go ahead. Look how few agents come up. Maybe one. Probably none posting consistently.

**TIM:** *(looking at phone)* Yeah. There's nobody.

**D.J.:** That's your answer. Even if only a sliver of your town is on there, you'll be the only agent talking to them. A small audience with zero competition beats a huge audience where you're shouting over nine in ten agents on Facebook. So today's action step is that search. Search your town, see the empty stage with your own eyes, and let that be the thing that gets you to post.

### SEGMENT 5: THE MATH/MOTIVATION (~12:45 – ~14:15)

**D.J.:** Let me put real numbers on what waiting costs you. Three posts a week is about a hundred and fifty videos in a year. On a platform where your local competition is basically zero.

**TIM:** A hundred and fifty swings of the bat, and almost nobody else is even at the plate.

**D.J.:** And the algorithm rewards the account with history. So the agent who starts this month owns that local real estate corner a year from now. The agent who waits is starting from zero next June while somebody else has a year of trust banked.

**TIM:** So it's not really about one video flopping.

**D.J.:** It's never about one video. The cost is the year you didn't start. A hundred million people a day, the cheapest lead source you'll ever touch, and the only thing standing between you and it is being willing to look a little silly for thirty seconds. That's the whole price. Most agents won't pay it. That's your opening.

### CLOSE (~14:15 – ~15:00)

**D.J.:** So here it is. Empty stage, packed room, three moves. Answer, plant the flag, get your reps. And that question you've been holding this whole time. Here's exactly what you do with it, right now, before you do anything else today. Open your phone. Camera on, vertical. Say your name, say your town, and answer that one question in thirty seconds. Don't edit it. Post it.

**TIM:** One question. Thirty seconds. No edit.

**D.J.:** And here's the thing. You're not "trying to become a TikTok person." That's the old story, and it's keeping you off the stage. The agents who win this aren't performers. They're the local expert who answers questions out loud where everybody can see. You just did that. That's the whole job.

**TIM:** That's it. Go film it.

**D.J.:** A hundred million people are in the room, and eighty-five percent of your competition is still standing outside. Quit watching from the wings. We'll see you next week.

**END**
