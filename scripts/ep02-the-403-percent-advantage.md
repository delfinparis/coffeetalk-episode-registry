# Coffee Talk with Tim & D.J.
## Episode 2: "The 403% Advantage Hiding in Your Pocket"
**Air Date:** 2026-02-16 | **Runtime:** 15 minutes | **Format:** Live stream | **Tone:** Two buddies having coffee, dropping truth bombs

## COLD OPEN (0:00 – ~0:45)

**D.J.:** (looking at phone, then up at camera) 403 percent.

**TIM:** 403 percent more... what? Commissions? Followers?

**D.J.:** Inquiries. 403% more inquiries on a listing. That's not a typo. That's four times the phone calls, four times the emails, four times the "hey, is this still available?"

**TIM:** From what? What are these agents doing differently?

**D.J.:** One thing. One single thing that 91% of agents refuse to do — even though their phone can do it for free in sixty seconds.

**TIM:** (pause) Alright. What is it?

**D.J.:** I'm D.J. Paris, that's Tim Littleton, and today we're going to show you the easiest competitive advantage in real estate right now. It's hiding in your pocket. Literally. And by the end of this episode, you're going to use it.

## SEGMENT 1: THE PROBLEM (~0:45 – ~3:30)

**TIM:** Okay. 403% more inquiries. From one thing. Spill it.

**D.J.:** Video. Listing video.

**TIM:** (sits back) Video.

**D.J.:** I know. You've heard it before. "You should do video." But here's what nobody tells you — the actual numbers behind it. NAR and multiple industry studies all land in the same place. Listings with video receive 403% more inquiries. Homes with video tours sell up to 31% faster. And here's the kicker —

**TIM:** Hit me.

**D.J.:** 73% of homeowners say they are more likely to list with an agent who uses video.

**TIM:** 73% of sellers want it.

**D.J.:** Want it. Prefer it. Would choose the video agent over the non-video agent. Nearly three out of four potential listing clients.

**TIM:** So what percentage of agents are actually doing it?

**D.J.:** Nine.

**TIM:** Nine percent?

**D.J.:** Nine percent of agents create listing videos. Nine.

**TIM:** (shaking head) So 73% of sellers want video... and 9% of agents offer it.

**D.J.:** That's a 64-point gap between what clients want and what agents deliver. Think about any other industry where that would be acceptable. Imagine 73% of diners saying "I prefer restaurants with menus" and 91% of restaurants going, "Nah, just trust us."

**TIM:** (laughing) That's insane.

**D.J.:** It is insane. And it gets worse. They also surveyed recent sellers — people who just sold a home. Asked them if their agent used video effectively. Ten percent said yes.

**TIM:** Ten percent.

**D.J.:** So not only are most agents not making video — the ones who are aren't even doing it well enough for sellers to notice.

**TIM:** Okay but here's what I know everyone watching is thinking. "I know I should do video. I've heard this." So why aren't they doing it?

**D.J.:** That's exactly where this gets interesting. Because the reasons agents give are the same three reasons — and all three are wrong.

---

## SEGMENT 2: WHY IT'S NOT HAPPENING (~3:30 – ~6:30)

**TIM:** Alright. 3 reasons agents don't do video. Let's hear them.

**D.J.:** Reason one. "I'm not good on camera."

**TIM:** That's the big one. And we hear this repeatedly.

**D.J.:** It's the biggest one. And I get it. Nobody likes hearing their own voice. Nobody thinks they look right on camera. But here's what's interesting — buyers don't care about production quality.

**TIM:** Really?

**D.J.:** A 2026 marketing trends report found that the most successful agent accounts mix professional listing photos with behind-the-scenes phone videos. The polished stuff AND the imperfect stuff. Buyers respond to authenticity. They want to see a real person walking through a real house — not a Hollywood production.

**TIM:** So the thing agents are afraid of — looking unprofessional — is actually what buyers respond to?

**D.J.:** The irony is beautiful. "Just lost a bidding war by $5K" gets more engagement than "Just closed another deal!" People connect with real. Not polished.

**TIM:** What's reason 2?

**D.J.:** "It takes too long." Agents are busy. They're running to showings, managing transactions, putting out fires. Adding video production to the list feels overwhelming.

**TIM:** And that's valid. Time is the one thing agents don't have a lot of.

**D.J.:** It's valid if you're thinking about video the old way. Hiring a videographer. Scheduling a shoot. Editing. Uploading. Yeah, that's a whole production. But that's not the only way to do video — and honestly, it's not even the most effective way anymore. The phone in your pocket shoots 4K. You can film a 60-second walkthrough during a showing you're already at.

**TIM:** You're already there!

**D.J.:** You're already standing in the house! You're already walking through the rooms! It takes sixty seconds to narrate what you're seeing.

**TIM:** And reason 3?

**D.J.:** "I don't know what to say."

**TIM:** That's the same thing we heard last week with follow-up.

**D.J.:** Exact same problem. And the fix is the same — you need a system. A repeatable template you don't have to think about. Which is exactly what we're going to give you. But first —

**TIM:** Here it comes.

**D.J.:** I want everyone watching to do something right now. Go find a mirror. Right now. And just... look at it.

**TIM:** (laughs) Just look at it?

**D.J.:** Just look at yourself staring back, even if you're not looking your best. That's your 403% advantage right there staring at you.

**TIM:** The person staring back is absolutely good enough to make videos.

**D.J.:** (sips coffee)

**D.J.:** Good. Now keep your phone nearby. Because as soon as this video is over, you're going to record a video. And I promise — it's going to be easier than you think.

## SEGMENT 3: THE 3-2-1 SYSTEM (~6:30 – ~10:30)

**TIM:** Alright. You promised a system. What is it?

**D.J.:** I call it the 3-2-1 System. Three types of videos. Two minutes or less. One take.

**TIM:** One take? No editing?

**D.J.:** No editing. That's the whole point. If the system requires editing, agents won't do it. If it requires equipment, agents won't do it. If it takes more than five minutes, agents won't do it. So the 3-2-1 System is designed around what agents will actually do.

**TIM:** Okay. What are the 3 types?

**D.J.:** Type one: The Walkthrough. This is your listing video. You're already in the house — either for a showing, a preview, an open house, whatever. You take your phone, hit record, and walk through. Narrate as you go.

**TIM:** What do you say?

**D.J.:** You say what you'd say if a friend asked about the house. "Hey, this is 432 Oak Street. Check out this kitchen — they just redid these countertops. And wait till you see this backyard." That's it. You're a tour guide, not a narrator.

**TIM:** No script.

**D.J.:** No script. You know the house. You know what's cool about it. Just say that while you walk through. Sixty to ninety seconds. Done.

**TIM:** Type two?

**D.J.:** The Market Minute. Once a week, you record a 60-second video about one thing happening in your market. It's not a full market report. It's one number, one trend, one insight.

**TIM:** Like what?

**D.J.:** "Hey, quick market minute for you — inventory in the north side dropped 12% this month. If you've been waiting to sell over there, the window is open. Call me if you want to know what your place is worth." That's the whole video. Thirty seconds. Maybe forty-five.

**TIM:** And that positions you as the local expert.

**D.J.:** Without writing a 2,000-word blog post that nobody reads. You're the person who knows the numbers. You show up in their feed every week saying something useful. When they're ready to move, who do you think they call?

**TIM:** What's the third type?

**D.J.:** The Behind-the-Scenes. This is the most underrated one. You film a moment from your real day. Staging a house. Previewing a new listing. Celebrating at a closing. Walking through a neighborhood. Just moments that show people what being a real estate agent actually looks like.

**TIM:** Why does that work?

**D.J.:** Because 51% of homebuyers are already on YouTube researching. They're watching. They're forming opinions about agents before they ever make a call. And the agents who show up on camera — showing their personality, showing their expertise, showing their day — those are the agents who feel familiar before the first meeting.

**TIM:** You're building trust before they ever call you.

**D.J.:** By the time they sit down with you, they already feel like they know you. That's an unfair advantage. And your competition — 91% of them — isn't doing it.

**TIM:** Three types. Two minutes max. One take.

**D.J.:** The 3-2-1 System. Now — remember I told you to keep your phone nearby?

**TIM:** Get ready to record...

**D.J.:** Here we go. In a few minutes this video will end and I want you to record a Market Minute. Don't overthink it. Here's your template. Ready?

**TIM:** Give it to them.

**D.J.:** Hold your phone up. Hit record. Say this: "Hey — quick market update for [your area]. [One fact about your local market right now]. If you have questions about what this means for your home, send me a message." Stop recording. That's your first video.

**TIM:** Seriously. You can record it in under 60 seconds. Don't have a market stat? Go to ChatGPT and ask for the most relevant for buyers and sellers in your area today.

**D.J.:** This video doesn't have to be perfect. It has to exist. A bad video posted beats a perfect video that lives in your head.

**(beat)**

**TIM:** Commit to doing this.

**D.J.:** And after you post it, it puts you ahead of 91% of every agent in your market. That's the bar. It's on the floor.

## SEGMENT 4: THE OBJECTIONS (~10:30 – ~12:45)

**TIM:** Okay. I'm going to push back because I know the audience is thinking this.

**D.J.:** Go for it.

**TIM:** "D.J., my market is different. My clients are older. They don't watch videos."

**D.J.:** I hear this constantly. And the data says otherwise. The largest group of homebuyers right now is Gen X — 24% of all buyers. Followed by older millennials at 21%, then younger boomers at 19%. These are not teenagers on TikTok. These are people in their 40s, 50s, and 60s who absolutely watch video online.

**TIM:** What about the "I'll look stupid" worry?

**D.J.:** Here's the thing I want everyone to hear. Nobody is watching your video to judge your hair or your voice. They're watching because they want to see the house. Or they want market information. You are the delivery vehicle, not the product. The house is the product. The information is the product. You just have to show up and deliver it.

**TIM:** What about agents who say "I tried video and it didn't really work for me"?

**D.J.:** How long did they try?

**TIM:** Probably a couple of videos.

**D.J.:** Right. They posted two videos, got 47 views, and said "video doesn't work." That's like going to the gym twice and saying "exercise doesn't work."

**TIM:** (laughs) Fair.

**D.J.:** Consistency beats quality every single time. One video per week for six months will transform your business more than one perfect video every three months. The algorithm rewards consistency. Your audience rewards consistency. You have to give people a chance to find you.

**TIM:** So the action step here is...

**D.J.:** Pick one day per week. Just one. That's your video day. Every Tuesday — or whatever day works — you record and post one video using the 3-2-1 System. Put it in your calendar right now. Recurring event. Every week. "Record and post one video."

**TIM:** Same advice as last week. If it's not on the calendar, it doesn't exist.

**D.J.:** Exactly. Systems beat intentions.

---

## SEGMENT 5: THE MATH (~12:45 – ~14:15)

**TIM:** Let's do the math. Because this is where it gets real.

**D.J.:** Okay. Average agent gets maybe 5 to 10 listing inquiries per listing through traditional marketing. Photos on MLS. Maybe a social media post.

**TIM:** That sounds about right.

**D.J.:** Now apply the 403% multiplier. Even if you're conservative and say video triples your inquiries instead of quadrupling them — you're going from 5 inquiries to 15. Per listing.

**TIM:** That's 10 more potential buyers seeing your listing. Per listing.

**D.J.:** Per listing. And those aren't just tire-kickers. People who watch video are 174% more likely to purchase than people who don't. They're more qualified. They're further down the decision path.

**TIM:** So more leads AND better leads.

**D.J.:** Now multiply that across a year. Say you have 10 listings. That's potentially 100 additional qualified inquiries you're not getting right now. Some of those become buyers. Some of those buyers refer you. Some of those sellers come back.

**TIM:** And the cost?

**D.J.:** Zero dollars. Zero equipment. About 15 minutes per week of time you're already spending in houses and neighborhoods.

**TIM:** So an agent doing 10 deals a year is potentially leaving — what — three, four, five additional deals on the table?

**D.J.:** At least. And that's just from listing video. We haven't even talked about the brand-building effect of the Market Minutes and the Behind-the-Scenes content. Those bring you listings you wouldn't have gotten in the first place.

**TIM:** So it's not just about selling this house faster. It's about getting the next listing.

**D.J.:** Because when a seller is interviewing agents, and one of them has a YouTube channel with 50 walkthrough videos and the other has... a headshot and a business card... who do you think gets the listing?

**TIM:** That's not even a competition.

**D.J.:** It's not. And right now, 91% of agents are bringing a business card to a video fight.

---

## CLOSE (~14:15 – ~15:00)

**TIM:** Alright. Let's land it. Recap time.

**D.J.:** 73% of sellers want an agent who uses video. Only 9% of agents do it. Listings with video get 403% more inquiries.

**TIM:** The system: 3-2-1. Three types of video — Walkthrough, Market Minute, Behind-the-Scenes. Two minutes or less. One take, no editing.

**D.J.:** Three action steps. One: record a Market Minute.

**TIM:** Two: Pick your video day. Put a recurring event in your calendar. One video per week. Non-negotiable.

**D.J.:** Three: The next time you're standing in a listing, hit record and walk through it. Sixty seconds. Post it. Done.

**TIM:** 91% of agents will never do this.

**D.J.:** Be the 9% who does.

**TIM:** Your clients are already watching video. The only question is — are they watching yours?

**D.J.:** The best camera is the one you already own. The best video is the one you actually post.

**TIM:** See you next week on Coffee Talk!

**D.J.:** Now go hit record.

**Both raise coffee cups**

**BOTH:** Cheers.
