# Coffee Talk with Tim & D.J.
## Episode 4: "The 5-Minute Rule"
**Air Date:** 2026-03-04 | **Runtime:** 15 minutes | **Format:** Live stream | **Tone:** Two buddies having coffee, dropping truth bombs

---

## COLD OPEN (0:00 – ~0:45)

**D.J.:** (sets down coffee, looks directly at camera) A hundred times.

**TIM:** A 100 times what?

**D.J.:** That's how much more likely you are to reach a lead if you call them back within five minutes instead of thirty.

**TIM:** A 100 times more likely.

**D.J.:** A hundred. Not ten. Not twice. A hundred. And the average real estate agent responds to an online lead in fifteen hours.

**TIM:** (slowly) Fifteen hours.

**D.J.:** I'm D.J. Paris, that's Tim Littleton — this is Coffee Talk. Today we're talking about the silent killer of your lead pipeline. It's not your scripts. It's not your marketing budget. It's a clock. And it's already running on every lead you have right now.

---

## SEGMENT 1: THE PROBLEM (~0:45 – ~3:30)

**TIM:** Okay, you said a hundred times. Where does that come from?

**D.J.:** MIT. Harvard Business Review published it. They analyzed over a million leads across industries, and the finding was consistent: respond within five minutes, you're a hundred times more likely to actually reach that lead and have a real conversation versus responding at the thirty-minute mark.

**TIM:** I want to push back on that. Because that sounds insane.

**D.J.:** Push back.

**TIM:** A hundred times feels like marketing math. Like someone needed a scary number.

**D.J.:** Here's why it's real. Think about what's happening in the moment someone fills out a form. They're sitting there, phone in hand, they just took an action. That's peak motivation. They're mentally available. They're expecting something to happen. Every minute after that, their attention fragments. They get a text. They start dinner. Their motivation cools. By hour fifteen—

**TIM:** They've forgotten they even submitted the form.

**D.J.:** Or they talked to two other agents who did call back fast, and they've already scheduled showings.

**TIM:** And NAR says — what, 96% of buyers are starting online now?

**D.J.:** 96% use the internet somewhere in their search. That's not a niche anymore. That's almost everyone.

**TIM:** So this isn't just a "Zillow lead" problem. This is the whole pipeline.

**D.J.:** And here's the line that I want agents to really sit with: you're not losing to better agents. You're losing to faster ones.

**TIM:** (shaking head) That one's going to hurt some people.

**D.J.:** It should. Because skill, reputation, market knowledge — none of that gets activated if you're not the one who picked up the phone first. Drop "LATE" in the comments if this has happened to you — you found out a lead you had went with someone else, and it still stings.

**TIM:** Because it's such a fixable problem.

**D.J.:** Completely fixable. And that's exactly where we're going next.

---

## SEGMENT 2: WHY IT HAPPENS (~3:30 – ~6:30)

**TIM:** So why? Why are agents waiting fifteen hours? Is it intentional? They're not trying to blow the lead.

**D.J.:** Three reasons. First — nobody told them the window exists. They don't know about the five-minute clock. They treat a lead like a voicemail. It'll keep. It won't.

**TIM:** It's like a souffle. You don't come back to it later.

**D.J.:** (laughs) Exactly. Second — lead notifications are scattered everywhere. Your CRM emails you. The app pings you. Zillow texts you. But you're in a showing. You're in a listing appointment. You're at your kid's soccer game. By the time you see it—

**TIM:** Three hours gone.

**D.J.:** At minimum. And the third reason — this is the one people don't want to admit — agents wait until they're ready. They want to pull up the address. Research the listing. Know who they're talking to. Be prepared.

**TIM:** Which sounds professional.

**D.J.:** It sounds professional and it costs you the deal. Because the lead doesn't care that you know the school district. They care that you're alive and paying attention. Agents are optimizing for the quality of the call when they should be optimizing for the speed of the call.

**TIM:** So you're saying call before you know anything?

**D.J.:** I'm saying call before the clock runs out and then learn everything. After the show, we want you to do one thing: pull up your last five leads — wherever they live, your CRM, your email, your Zillow app — and look at the gap between when the lead came in and when you first responded. Write that number down. Because the system we're about to show you exists to change it.

**TIM:** That number is going to mean something different to you by the end of this show.

**D.J.:** What's the system called?

**TIM:** The 5-Minute Rule. Three components. Let's build it.

---

## SEGMENT 3: THE SYSTEM/SOLUTION (~6:30 – ~11:00)

**D.J.:** The 5-Minute Rule. Three components. Each one takes about ten minutes to set up once. Together they solve the problem permanently.

**TIM:** Hit me with component one.

**D.J.:** Auto-Acknowledge. The second a lead hits — any source, any form, any platform — they get an automated text. Not email. Text. Something like: "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] at [Brokerage]. Just saw your request — I'll be calling you in the next few minutes." That's it. Short. Human. No pitch.

**TIM:** So they know you're real before you even call.

**D.J.:** They know you're real, they know you're fast, and now they're expecting your call. Meanwhile most agents are sending nothing — or a generic email that goes to spam. You've already separated yourself and you haven't said a single word live.

**TIM:** How do you actually set that up though? Like technically, for someone who's not super CRM-savvy?

**D.J.:** Every major CRM — Follow Up Boss, LionDesk, kvCORE, Sierra — they all have automated text triggers. You set a rule: lead comes in, text fires. Google "[your CRM] automated lead text trigger" and it's a ten-minute YouTube tutorial. Set it once. Runs forever.

**TIM:** Okay. Component two.

**D.J.:** The Live Call. This is the actual five-minute window. When the lead hits, your job is simple: stop what you're doing and call. Not in a few minutes. Now. And here's the script — because agents always ask about the script. Ready?

**TIM:** Give it to them.

**D.J.:** "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] — I just got your request and wanted to reach out right away. Is now a good time for two minutes?"

**TIM:** Two minutes? You're not pitching them?

**D.J.:** The goal of the first call is to schedule the second call. That's it. You're not trying to close them. You're trying to make them feel like submitting that form was a smart move. The whole vibe is: I'm responsive, I'm real, let's talk when you have time.

**TIM:** That actually takes the pressure off. You don't have to have your whole pitch ready.

**D.J.:** You don't need to know anything about them yet. Speed is the pitch.

**TIM:** Component three.

**D.J.:** The 3-Touch Sequence. Because most of them won't answer the first call. And most agents give up after one attempt.

**TIM:** How many attempts are we talking?

**D.J.:** Research says it takes an average of six touches to reach a lead. Most agents make 1.3.

**TIM:** One and a third.

**D.J.:** One call, no answer, lead goes cold forever. Here's the sequence that actually works: Call immediately — that's your live call. Text right after: "Hey, just tried to reach you — happy to connect whenever works for you." Call again that evening. If still nothing — email day two, brief and human. One final call day three. After that, long-term nurture. You've done your job.

**TIM:** And that's your whole system. Auto-Acknowledge, Live Call, 3-Touch Sequence.

**D.J.:** The 5-Minute Rule. And that gap number we mentioned earlier — the time between when your leads came in and when you called? That number is your current cost. This system is the fix. Drop "FIVE" in the comments if you're implementing this.

---

## SEGMENT 4: THE HARD OBJECTION (~11:00 – ~12:45)

**TIM:** Here's the thing though. I know a lot of agents — good agents, busy agents — who don't work online leads at all. Their whole business is their sphere. Referrals. Past clients. They don't have Zillow forms coming in. Is any of this relevant to them, or can they honestly tune out?

**D.J.:** Honest answer: the literal framework — setting up CRM text triggers for online leads — no, that's not their issue.

**TIM:** So they can check out.

**D.J.:** They cannot check out. Because here's the question I'd ask every referral-based agent: when someone texts you asking to see a house — a person your past client sent you — how fast do you respond?

**TIM:** (pause) Oh.

**D.J.:** The 5-Minute Rule isn't just for online leads. It's a responsiveness standard. When your past client said "call my agent," and then that person texts you and waits two hours for a reply — your past client looks bad. You look bad. And you will never get another referral from that person.

**TIM:** So the referral agent is actually at more risk of blowing this. Because it's a relationship on the line, not just a cold lead.

**D.J.:** Exactly. Here's the specific fix for referral-based agents: create what I call a Response SLA for yourself. Business hours, any inbound inquiry — text, call, referral, anything — gets a response within five minutes. And in your phone contacts for every active referral source, put a note: "5-min SLA." When they send you someone, you see that note, the clock is already in your head.

**TIM:** So the principle is universal. Speed is respect.

**D.J.:** Speed is respect. Whether it's a Zillow form or your best client's neighbor — someone reached out. The clock started. Five minutes.

---

## SEGMENT 5: THE MATH/MOTIVATION (~12:45 – ~14:15)

**D.J.:** Let's put dollars to this. Average agent, mid-range market. Average commission around $8,000. You're generating ten online leads a month — maybe through your site, Zillow, ads, whatever.

**TIM:** Ten leads a month.

**D.J.:** At a 15-hour average response time, you're converting maybe one of those. Maybe two on a good month. That's the real-world math.

**TIM:** One or two deals out of ten leads.

**D.J.:** Conservative estimate — implement the 5-Minute Rule, you go from one to three conversions. Studies on response time consistently show conversion rates jump two to four times with fast follow-up. Call it two extra deals a month.

**TIM:** Two extra deals at eight grand each.

**D.J.:** Sixteen thousand dollars a month. Not from more leads. Not from a bigger marketing budget. From calling back faster.

**TIM:** That's $192,000 a year.

**D.J.:** And flip it. Every month you don't have this system, you're leaving sixteen thousand dollars on the table. Not hypothetically. Actually. The leads came in. You paid for them — through your Zillow subscription, your ad spend, your time building your brand. The pipeline is generating. You're just not picking it up.

**TIM:** The leads aren't broken.

**D.J.:** The response is broken. And that's fixable today.

---

## CLOSE (~14:15 – ~15:00)

**D.J.:** Three things to do the second you close this app. Tim?

**TIM:** One.

**D.J.:** Pull up your last five leads and find your gap number — the time between when each lead came in and when you first responded. Write it down. That's your baseline. That's what you're fixing.

**TIM:** Two.

**D.J.:** Open your CRM and set up your Auto-Acknowledge text trigger. Lead comes in, text fires automatically. It's a ten-minute setup. Google "[your CRM] automated lead text trigger" and you'll have it done before your next cup of coffee.

**TIM:** Three.

**D.J.:** Write down your live call script — "Just got your request, is now a good time for two minutes?" — and build your 3-Touch Sequence as a workflow in your CRM. Call, text, call, email, call. Fifteen minutes. Done forever.

**TIM:** Here's the truth: 97% of agents watching this are going to nod and move on. Be the 3% who actually do it. That's not a crowded club. And your competition is counting on you staying in the majority.

**D.J.:** (raises coffee cup) Every agent has a gap number. From today, yours is five minutes. First one in wins.

**TIM:** That's Coffee Talk. See you next week.

Both raise coffee cups.

**BOTH:** Cheers.
