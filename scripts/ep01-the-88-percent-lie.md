# Coffee Talk with Tim & D.J.
## Episode 1: "The 88% Lie: Why Your Past Clients Aren't Coming Back"
**Air Date:** 2026-02-11 | **Runtime:** 15 minutes | **Format:** Live stream | **Tone:** Two buddies having coffee, dropping truth bombs

---

## COLD OPEN (0:00 – ~0:45)

**D.J.:** (setting down coffee, looking directly at camera) 88 percent.

**TIM:** 88 percent of what?

**D.J.:** That's the number everyone loves to throw around. "88% of past clients say they'd use their agent again." Feels good, right?

**TIM:** Sounds like a win to me.

**D.J.:** It's a lie. Well — not a lie. It's a half-truth. And the other half? That's what's costing agents thousands of dollars every single year.

**TIM:** (leaning in) Alright. You've got my attention.

**D.J.:** I'm D.J. Paris, that's Tim Littleton, and today we're exposing the biggest gap between what your clients say and what they actually do. If you've ever wondered why your phone isn't ringing with repeat business — stay right here. We're about to fix it.

---

## SEGMENT 1: THE PROBLEM (~0:45 – ~3:30)

**TIM:** Okay, so 88% of clients say they'd use their agent again. Where's the lie?

**D.J.:** The lie is in what actually happens next. Researchers tracked what clients did — not what they said in a survey. And only 12 to 15 percent actually used the same agent again.

**TIM:** (pause) Hold on. 88% say they would...

**D.J.:** And 12% do.

**TIM:** That's a 70-point gap.

**D.J.:** More than 70 points. Between intention and action.

**TIM:** So we've got almost 9 out of 10 clients saying "yeah, you're my agent for life" — and then they go hire someone else?

**D.J.:** They hire someone else. They forget your name. They Google "realtor near me" three years later because they can't remember who helped them buy their house.

**TIM:** That's painful. But why? If they were happy with the service, why wouldn't they come back?

**D.J.:** Here's where it gets brutal. NAR did a study. 91% of real estate agents never contact their clients after closing.

**TIM:** 91%?

**D.J.:** 91%. You close the deal. You hand them the keys. You shake hands. And then... nothing. Radio silence. For months. For years. Forever.

**TIM:** And then we wonder why the phone doesn't ring.

**D.J.:** We wonder why our past clients hired their neighbor's cousin's friend instead of calling us.

**TIM:** So the 88% number isn't a victory. It's actually a tragedy. Because the clients want to come back. They intend to come back. They just...

**D.J.:** They forget. You disappear from their life, and someone else shows up when they're ready to move again. That Zillow ad. That agent doing neighborhood videos. That mailer that landed the same week they decided to sell.

**TIM:** Out of sight, out of mind.

**D.J.:** Out of sight, out of wallet.

---

## SEGMENT 2: WHY IT HAPPENS (~3:30 – ~6:30)

**TIM:** Okay, so 91% of agents go "dark" after closing. But I know these people. They're not lazy. They're not trying to lose business. So what's happening?

**D.J.:** Three things. And I guarantee everyone watching has felt all three.

**TIM:** Let's hear them.

**D.J.:** Number one: The next fire. The deal closes, and there's immediately another crisis. Another showing. Another negotiation. Another client who needs you right now. Post-closing follow-up isn't urgent. So it gets pushed.

**TIM:** And "pushed" means "never."

**D.J.:** Pushed means never. Number two: You don't know what to say.

**TIM:** This is real.

**D.J.:** It's so real. The transaction is over. There's no "reason" to call. So you feel awkward. Like you're being salesy. Like you're bothering them.

**TIM:** "Hey, just calling to check on you" feels weird when you haven't talked in 8 months.

**D.J.:** It feels weird because you're thinking about yourself. "What do I say? What's my excuse for calling?" But here's the flip — what if you thought about them instead? "What would actually be helpful to them right now?"

**TIM:** Different question.

**D.J.:** Completely different question. And number three — this is the big one.

**TIM:** Give it to me.

**D.J.:** No system. Most agents have good intentions. They might even have a CRM gathering dust somewhere. But there's no rhythm. No calendar. No "every past client gets contacted X times per year, period."

**TIM:** It's random. It's whenever you remember. Which means...

**D.J.:** Which means it's basically never.

**TIM:** Alright. So we've got the diagnosis. What's the prescription?

**D.J.:** We're getting there. But first — I want everyone watching to do something right now.

**TIM:** Right now?

**D.J.:** Right now. Open your phone. Go to your contacts. Scroll until you find a past client you haven't talked to in at least six months. Just find their name. Don't text them yet. Just find them.

**TIM:** We'll tell you exactly what to say in a few minutes. But right now, just locate one person.

**D.J.:** (takes a sip of coffee) Do it. We're waiting.

**TIM:** (beat) Got someone?

**D.J.:** Good. Keep that name in your head. That person is going to get a text from you before this episode is over.

---

## SEGMENT 3: THE 4-4-4 SYSTEM (~6:30 – ~10:30)

**TIM:** Alright, so we know the problem. We know why it happens. Now let's fix it. What is the system?

**D.J.:** I call it the 4-4-4 System. It's stupid simple. That's the point. If it's complicated, you won't do it.

**TIM:** Break it down for us.

**D.J.:** First four: Four touches per year. That's your minimum. Once per quarter. If you contact every past client just four times a year, you are already ahead of 91% of agents.

**TIM:** 4 times a year. That doesn't sound like much.

**D.J.:** It's not. But most agents aren't even doing that. So the bar is on the ground. Step over it and you win.

**TIM:** What's the second 4?

**D.J.:** Four categories of touches. This solves the "I don't know what to say" problem. You rotate through four types.

**TIM:** What are they?

**D.J.:** Category one: The Check-In. This is the simplest. No agenda. Just "Hey, how's the house? Anything you need?" That's it.

**TIM:** You're not asking for business.

**D.J.:** You're not asking for anything. You're just being a human who gives a damn. And nine times out of ten, they'll say "all good!" But that tenth time? "Actually, we've been thinking about moving." "My brother's relocating here." Boom. You're in the conversation.

**TIM:** Category two?

**D.J.:** The Value Drop. You send them something useful. A neighborhood market update. An article about home maintenance. A heads-up about property tax deadlines. Something that positions you as "I'm still your real estate resource."

**TIM:** Not salesy. Just helpful!

**D.J.:** Just helpful. You're staying in their life as the person who knows things. So when they have a question, you're the first call.

**TIM:** Third category?

**D.J.:** The Celebration. This one's underrated and almost nobody does it. You acknowledge something in their life. Their home anniversary. Their birthday if you know it. Kid's graduation. Anything that says "I see you as a human, not a commission check."

**TIM:** When's the last time any business you worked with sent you a note on your anniversary with them?

**D.J.:** Never. That's why when you do it, it lands.

**TIM:** What's the fourth category?

**D.J.:** The Ask. Once per year — only once — you ask directly. "Hey, I'm growing my business through referrals this year. If you know anyone thinking about buying or selling, I'd be grateful for an introduction."

**TIM:** Isn't that awkward?

**D.J.:** Not if you've done the other three first. You've already checked in. You've already added value. You've already celebrated them. So when you ask, it doesn't feel like a grab. It feels earned.

**TIM:** Check-in. Value drop. Celebration. Ask. Once a quarter. That's the system.

**D.J.:** That's the first two fours. Now here's the third four: Four minutes or less per touch.

**TIM:** Per touch?

**D.J.:** Per touch. This isn't writing love letters. A quick text. A short voicemail. A two-line email. You can do this while you're waiting for your coffee order.

**TIM:** So if I have 50 past clients, that's...

**D.J.:** 200 touches per year. At four minutes each, that's about 13 hours total. For the entire year.

**TIM:** 13 hours to stay top of mind with everyone who already trusts you.

**D.J.:** Versus how many hours do agents spend chasing strangers who don't know them and don't trust them?

**TIM:** (laughs) A lot more than 13!

**D.J.:** A lot more than 13. Alright — remember that person you found earlier? The past client you haven't talked to?

**TIM:** I've got mine.

**D.J.:** Here's what you're going to do. Right now. I'm going to give you the exact text to send. Ready?

**TIM:** Give it to them.

**D.J.:** "Hey [Name] — was just thinking about you. How's the house treating you? Hope you're doing well."

**TIM:** That's it?

**D.J.:** That's it. No pitch. No agenda. No "let me know if you know anyone buying or selling." Just a human being checking in.

**TIM:** Send it. Right now! Before we keep going.

**D.J.:** We're serious. Hit pause if you have to. Copy that message. Personalize the name. Send it.

**TIM:** (takes a sip of coffee, waits)

**D.J.:** And if you want us to know you did it, drop "SENT" in the comments. Let's see how many of you are taking action.

**TIM:** We're watching those comments.

---

## SEGMENT 4: MAKING IT STICK (~10:30 – ~12:45)

**D.J.:** Okay. You sent the text. Congratulations — you just did more than 91% of agents do in a year.

**TIM:** (laughs) That's a pretty low bar.

**D.J.:** It's a low bar. But here's the thing — one text isn't a system. So let's talk about making this stick.

**TIM:** Because I know what happens. You do it once, you feel good, and then three months go by and you're back to radio silence.

**D.J.:** Exactly. So here's the key: take it out of your head and put it into a system.

**TIM:** What does that look like?

**D.J.:** If you have a CRM — any CRM — set up a recurring task for every past client. Once per quarter, you get a reminder. "Contact Sarah Johnson." That's it.

**TIM:** What if you don't have a CRM?

**D.J.:** Use your calendar. Block one hour per week — same time every week — labeled "Past Client Outreach." Protect it like a showing. That hour is when you make your calls, send your texts, write your notes.

**TIM:** One hour a week.

**D.J.:** One hour a week is more than enough for most agents. And here's the thing — it's not extra work. It's the most leveraged work you can do. These people already trust you.

**TIM:** It's a warm outreach.

**D.J.:** It's the warmest outreach there is. No cold calls. No door knocking. No rejection. Just checking in on people who already like you.

**TIM:** What happens when they respond?

**D.J.:** Most of the time? Nothing dramatic. "Hey! Good to hear from you! House is great!" And you just... chat. Like a human. But sometimes — and this is where the money is — they say something like...

**TIM:** "Actually, we've been thinking about upgrading."

**D.J.:** Or "My parents need to downsize." Or "My coworker just moved here and asked if I knew an agent."

**TIM:** And you're in a real conversation.

**D.J.:** A conversation that never would have happened if you hadn't reached out. That's the business that's just sitting there. Waiting. The only thing between you and it is a text message.

**TIM:** What's the action step here?

**D.J.:** Before you go to bed tonight, do one of two things. Either set up quarterly reminders in your CRM for every past client. Or open your calendar and block one hour per week for past client outreach.

**TIM:** Pick one. Do it today.

**D.J.:** Not tomorrow. Today. Because tomorrow you'll forget. And then it's six more months of silence.

---

## SEGMENT 5: THE MATH THAT CHANGES EVERYTHING (~12:45 – ~14:15)

**TIM:** D.J., let's do the math on this. Because I think when people see the actual numbers, they'll never skip this again.

**D.J.:** Let's do it. Say you've been in the business five years. You've closed 50 transactions. Just 50 — that's 10 a year.

**TIM:** Pretty average.

**D.J.:** Pretty average. Now — 88% of those clients would theoretically use you again or refer you. That's 44 people who want to send you business.

**TIM:** 44 people.

**D.J.:** But here's what happens if you go silent like 91% of agents. You're only going to capture maybe 10-15% of that potential. That's 5 or 6 deals. From that entire group. Over the rest of your career.

**TIM:** That's it?

**D.J.:** That's it. But flip it. If you stay in touch — really stay in touch, four times a year, every year — and you capture 50% of that potential instead? Now you're looking at 22 transactions from that same group. Repeat business. Referrals. Their friends. Their family.

**TIM:** Four times the deals.

**D.J.:** From people you've already worked with. And here's the thing — every year you add more past clients. So the snowball keeps growing.

**TIM:** The agents doing $20, $30, $50 million — this is their secret, isn't it?

**D.J.:** This is a huge part of it. They're not out there grinding cold calls every day. They built a referral engine. And the fuel for that engine is staying in touch.

**TIM:** The fortune is in the follow-up.

**D.J.:** I know it's cliche. But it's cliche because it's true. It's not a cute saying. It's math. It's real money you're leaving on the table every month you don't reach out.

---

## CLOSE (~14:15 – ~15:00)

**TIM:** Alright, let's land this plane. Quick recap.

**D.J.:** 88% of past clients say they'd use you again. But only 12% actually do.

**TIM:** Why? Because 91% of agents never reach out after closing.

**D.J.:** The fix: The 4-4-4 System. Four touches per year. Four categories — check-in, value drop, celebration, ask. Four minutes or less per touch.

**TIM:** 3 action steps.

**D.J.:** One: You already did it. You texted a past client. If you didn't, do it right now. Pause this and do it.

**TIM:** 2: Set up the system. CRM reminders or a weekly calendar block. Before you go to bed tonight.

**D.J.:** Three: Write "4-4-4" somewhere you'll see it every day. Sticky note on your monitor. Reminder in your phone. Make it impossible to forget.

**TIM:** Your past clients already trust you. They already said they'd use you again.

**D.J.:** Don't let them forget you exist.

**TIM:** Be the 9% who actually follows up.

**D.J.:** Collect the business that's already yours.

**TIM:** See you next time on Coffee Talk.

**D.J.:** Now go check on your people.

*(both raise coffee cups)*

**BOTH:** Cheers.
